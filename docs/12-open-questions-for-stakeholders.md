# Open Questions For Stakeholders

## Governance And Ownership

- Who is the formal project owner?
- Who can approve data access and sharing?
- Who is the legal/privacy review contact?
- Who represents operational investigators or reviewers?
- What deliverable format is expected by the anti-fraud office and CIB?

## Data Availability

- Can confirmed scam-ad examples be provided?
- Can suspected but unconfirmed examples be provided?
- Can benign comparator examples be provided?
- Are screenshots, URLs, page/profile references, advertiser metadata, reports, or takedown status available?
- Can landing-page screenshots or redirect chains be shared?
- Are 165 report fields available for linking, aggregation, or taxonomy design?
- What fields must be redacted before research use?

## Platform And Legal Constraints

- Is automated collection from public Meta surfaces allowed under any written authorization?
- Is official API access available or planned?
- Are researchers allowed to manually capture public examples?
- What data retention period is allowed?
- Can examples include personal names, faces, comments, or profile identifiers?
- Can evidence be used in publications or only internal memos?

## Operational Priorities

- Which scam families matter most now: investment, fake e-commerce, healthcare, phishing, subscription traps, romance, job/task scams?
- Which platforms are most important: Facebook, Instagram, Threads?
- Are paid ads more important than organic scam-like posts?
- Is the goal prevention, triage, investigation support, policy evaluation, or procurement research?
- What reviewer capacity is realistic?

## Evaluation

- What counts as a confirmed scam?
- What counts as sufficient evidence for escalation?
- Who can adjudicate uncertain cases?
- Which false positives are most harmful?
- Which false negatives are most harmful?
- Is high-risk precision more important than broad recall in Phase 1?

## Fragile Assumptions

- That real examples can be shared.
- That landing-page evidence can be lawfully collected.
- That enough benign comparators can be collected.
- That reviewers can label consistently.
- That Meta-related ad evidence will include enough text/image/context fields.
- That NTD 1.8M is intended for research, not production enforcement.

## Questions For A First Meeting

1. What exact decision do you want this research to support after 8-12 weeks?
2. What data can you provide without new legal negotiation?
3. What would make this project successful even if no production system is built?
4. What should we explicitly avoid promising?
